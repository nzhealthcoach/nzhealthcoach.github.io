CIRS Labs

After looking at the biotoxin symptom clusters and taking the VCS test, the next step in the CIRS diagnostic process involves a number of lab tests.

Here's a summary of the recommended initial tests; they are explained in detail below.

  • ACTH and Cortisol
  • ADH (Vasopressin)
  • Osmolality
  • C3a
  • C4a
  • TGF beta-1
  • MMP-9
  • VEGF
  • HLA DR/DQ
  • MSH
  • Brain MRI with NeuroQuant

Adrenocorticotropic Hormone (ACTH) is produced and secreted by the pitutary gland. ACTH is produced in response to stress or exercise, and follows a regular circadian rhythm. It causes the production and release of cortisol by the adrenal glands.

Having low ACTH in relation to cortisol is common in CIRS.
